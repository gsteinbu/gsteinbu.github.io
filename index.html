I'm a Data Scientist

Data has many facettes, and so do I

What is a Data Scientist?

For me a data scientist is a statistician that focuses on application. Application in the realm of todays data means heavy utilization of computers. Since data is usually from a domain that has experts itself (like biology or engineering), a data scientist should also be proficient in communicating with these experts. Thus, I think a data scientist is the intersection of a statistician and a computer scientist with excellent communication skills.

Why am I a Data Scientist?

Put simply, I find data fascinating. Data is everywhere and, thus, is an entry point in almost all domains of our todays society — which pleases my curiosity. At the same time, data provides us with a form of evidence, which I find incredible important. For example, if you are arguing about what big or small effect on CO2 emission a general speed limit could have or you want to know how you might reduce your carbon footprint from food, data can guide you.
